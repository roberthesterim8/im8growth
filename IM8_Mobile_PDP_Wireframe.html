1
Announcement Bar with Live Countdown
CRITICAL
Combine the offer message with a live countdown timer directly in the bar. Currently IM8's bar is static ("SAVE 30% + GET FREE WELCOME GIFTS") — no timer creates zero urgency. The countdown should reset daily or reference an actual sale end date.
Every competitor with a timer uses it in the top bar: RYZE Grüns Instant Hydration Everyday Dose. Countdown timers are consistently cited as one of the highest-ROI conversion elements on supplement PDPs. RYZE's timer sits in the buy box itself — IM8 should do both.
→ Current IM8 gap: static bar with no scarcity signal
2
Minimal Nav — Logo + Cart Only
HIGH
On mobile, collapse the full navigation to just the logo and a cart icon. Remove "Shop", "Science", "The Beckham Stack" etc. from the PDP nav. Every click away from this page is a lost conversion. The full nav should only appear on non-PDP pages.
DIRTEA and RYZE both use minimal mobile nav on their PDPs. Reducing exit points is a proven mobile CRO principle — the PDP is a landing page, not a browsing experience.
→ Current IM8 gap: full multi-item nav takes up vertical space and offers escape routes
3
Hero Image — Single Full-Width, Swipeable Gallery
HIGH
Replace the current desktop-style left-rail thumbnail layout with a single full-width swipeable image carousel. On mobile, the thumbnail strip wastes space and images are too small to convert. First image = lifestyle/aspirational (the full gift set). Subsequent images = product close-up, ingredients, certifications, before/after lifestyle.

Add two overlay badges: a "BEST VALUE" pill top-left, and "30-DAY BACK" top-right. These reduce perceived risk at first glance.
All 6 competitors use single full-width hero images on mobile. The current IM8 desktop layout with a left thumbnail rail is unusable at 390px width.
→ Current IM8 gap: thumbnail gallery requires right-rail layout that doesn't translate to mobile
4
Social Proof Bar — Inline, One Line
HIGH
Keep the strong review stats but compress them to a single scannable horizontal bar directly below the image. Stars + rating + review count + pipe divider + purchase count + pipe + serving count. This is IM8's strongest trust asset — it should be impossible to miss.
AG1 puts "50,000+ verified 5-star AG1 reviews" immediately below the product image. RYZE shows "199,574 Reviews" in large bold type. The current IM8 implementation is good but sits at the very top of the right column on desktop — on mobile it gets lost.
→ Current IM8 asset: 4.8 from 16,225 Reviews | 730K+ purchases | 22M+ servings — excellent, just needs better placement
5
Short Product Title + Per-Serving Price Anchor
CRITICAL
Shorten the title from "Daily Ultimate Essentials Pro: All-in-One Supplement" to just "Daily Ultimate Essentials Pro". Add a punchy 1-line descriptor below.

Per-serving cost must be displayed prominently in red, immediately below the price. "£2.61 per serving · Less than a coffee" reframes £78/month as a trivially small daily decision. This is one of the most powerful price anchoring techniques in DTC health.
RYZE and Everyday Dose both display "$0.90 per serving" in large text next to their price. This single element measurably reduces sticker shock for monthly subscription products. Currently IM8 shows "£2.61 GBP / SERVING" only in very small grey text inside the subscription selector — it's invisible.
→ Current IM8 gap: per-serving cost buried in subscription box, not anchored near main price
6
Flavour Selector — Horizontal Pills, Not Dropdown
MEDIUM
Replace the current accordion-style flavour selector with horizontal tappable pill buttons. The "Variety Pack" option should be pre-selected and badged "BEST" or "RECOMMENDED." Other flavours are visible but de-emphasised. No extra tap needed to start the purchase flow.
RYZE uses horizontal tile selectors for both flavour and bundle. AG1 uses a simple two-button toggle. The accordion pattern on the current IM8 page adds friction — it requires a tap to reveal and a second tap to select.
→ Current IM8 gap: flavour selection is buried in an accordion, adds unnecessary friction
7
Bundle Tiles — All Options Visible, Savings Prominent
CRITICAL
Show all three purchasing options (90-Day, 30-Day, One-Time) as stacked visible tiles — no accordions, no hidden options. The 90-Day tile should be pre-selected and largest. Each tile must show: price per month, price per serving, % savings, and key included benefits as chips.

The One-Time option is intentionally less prominent (smaller, grey text) to guide toward subscription without hiding it — full transparency reduces buyer's remorse and increases trust.
Instant Hydration's bundle tiles (1 Box / 2 Boxes MOST POPULAR / 1+1 FREE BEST DEAL) are the clearest example. Grüns uses a similar "Subscribe & Save / One Time" layout. The key insight: making savings comparison easy at a glance increases AOV and subscription rate. Instant Hydration Grüns RYZE DIRTEA
→ Current IM8 gap: subscription options require scrolling within the purchase box; not immediately comparable
8
Gift Unlock Box — In-Line, Visual, Conditional
CRITICAL
Move the free gifts from the hero image into a dedicated "UNLOCKED" box that appears directly between the bundle selector and the CTA. Show each gift item with a small visual tile, its retail value struck through, and "FREE" in green. Show a running total: "£72 in gifts — yours free with the 90-Day plan."

This box should dynamically change: selecting the 30-Day plan shows fewer gifts, and the One-Time option shows none. This makes the subscription trade-off tangible and visible.
Everyday Dose and Instant Hydration both use an in-buy-box unlock mechanic. Everyday Dose's "CLAIM YOUR FREE STARTER KIT" section is essentially a second hero. The psychological principle: anchoring the gift value directly against the price makes the subscription feel like "getting paid to subscribe." Everyday Dose Instant Hydration AG1
→ Current IM8 gap: free gifts are in the hero image only, not repeated at the point of purchase decision
9
Primary CTA — Short, Action-Oriented, Includes Saving
CRITICAL
Change CTA from "START MY 90-DAY PROGRAM — £78.33 GBP/MO" to "GET STARTED — SAVE 30%" with a secondary badge inside the button showing "+ FREE GIFTS." The CTA should be full-width, bold, red, and large (min 52px height). Secondary "One-Time Purchase · £112" link below in smaller grey text.

Below the CTA: one line showing shipping destination + "Secure Checkout" reassurance. Not a wall of text.
Best CTA patterns observed: RYZE's "GET STARTED →", Everyday Dose's "TRY DOSE TODAY →", Instant Hydration's "SELECT FLAVORS $110 → $60.50." All are short, forward-leaning, and avoid leading with the price. The current IM8 CTA leads with cost — psychologically the worst order. RYZE Everyday Dose Grüns
10
Trust Trio — 3 Icons Directly Below CTA
HIGH
Three icon+label trust signals immediately below the CTA: 90-Day Money Back / Free Shipping / Cancel Anytime. These address the three most common objections for first-time supplement buyers. Keep them compact — icon, bold label, one-line description.
Grüns places "Clinically and 3rd party tested / 30-day guarantee / HSA/FSA eligible" as an icon row directly below their CTA. DIRTEA has "Free Shipping / Managing subscription anytime" as inline text below their add to cart. This is the universal pattern across all 6 competitors. Grüns DIRTEA RYZE
11
Benefits Strip — Outcomes, Not Ingredients
HIGH
Show 4 outcome-led benefit rows (energy, gut, sleep, focus) with the clinical percentages inline. Lead with the human outcome, not the ingredient name. Icon + bold outcome + one-line stat. This mirrors the clinical trial results but in benefit language, not scientific language.
AG1 uses "Sustained energy & focus / Immune defence / Supports regular digestion / Stress & mood support" with checkmarks immediately below the product description. Grüns lists "Promotes mental clarity + energy / Supports immunity + stress relief" as checked bullets. Benefits near the CTA outperform benefits far below the fold. AG1 Grüns
12
Urgency Banner with Countdown — Repeated Mid-Page
CRITICAL
Add a second urgency moment mid-page (after the trust trio and benefits) in a dark banner with a live countdown timer. This catches users who scrolled past the announcement bar without registering it. The dark background creates visual contrast and stops the scroll.
RYZE's countdown timer is built directly into their buy box — visible before and after scrolling. Grüns and Instant Hydration both place sale urgency at the very top of the page. The mid-page repeat is a recommended addition given IM8's longer page length.
→ New addition — not currently on the IM8 PDP at all
13
Expert Strip — Scrollable Avatars, Above Clinical Stats
HIGH
Move the expert grid much higher — from its current deep-page position to just below the urgency banner. Compress from a 2×3 grid to a horizontally scrollable avatar strip: circular photo + name + 1-line title. This is faster to scan and works well on mobile.
Everyday Dose's "662 clinicians share this on FrontrowMD" is placed near the top of their page. Grüns uses "Tested by Light Labs, 35 substances tested" early in the flow. Expert credibility should precede clinical data — establish authority first, then show the evidence.
→ Current IM8 gap: expert section is below the fold and too far down the page to influence the purchase decision
14
Clinical Stats — 2×2 Grid on Dark Background
HIGH
Keep the clinical stats section (95%/85%/80%/75%) but compress from 4-across to a 2×2 grid. The current desktop layout becomes 4 tiny cards in a row on mobile. Dark background with gold numbers creates strong visual impact and positions IM8 as premium/scientific. Add the research attribution footnote.
IM8 already has this section on the current PDP — it just needs mobile layout optimisation. The 4-column desktop layout breaks at mobile. The dark background + gold numbers aesthetic is highly on-brand.
→ Current IM8 asset: good content, needs mobile layout fix
15
NSF Cert + Celebrity Endorsement — Inline Trust Signals
MEDIUM
Show NSF Certified and the Aryna Sabalenka endorsement as compact list rows — icon/avatar + name + one-line credential. This is more scannable than a full-width section and keeps momentum through the page rather than stopping it.
Grüns uses "Clinically and 3rd party tested / 30-day guarantee / HSA/FSA eligible" as compact inline trust rows. The principle: late in the scroll, trust signals should be quick confirmations, not new information blocks.
16
UGC Video Row — Horizontal Scroll
MEDIUM
Keep the UGC ambassador video strip, but reduce it to a compact horizontal scroll of video thumbnails with play buttons. Show reviewer name and a one-word benefit tag per video (e.g. "Energy", "Gut Health"). Compress vertical height so it doesn't overwhelm the scroll.
IM8 already has strong UGC content — it just needs layout compression for mobile. The current implementation loads a full-height video carousel that takes up too much scroll distance.
→ Current IM8 asset: good content, needs mobile height compression
17
Ingredient Comparison Table — PRO vs Original
HIGH
Keep the ingredient comparison table — this is a genuine differentiator unique to IM8. Tighten the layout with a 3-column grid (Ingredient / Original / PRO Version) and use colour-coded upgrade tags (+733%, +300%, NEW, UPGRADE). Show 4–6 hero ingredients; add a "View full supplement facts panel" link.
None of the 6 competitors have an ingredient upgrade comparison table. This is IM8's strongest product differentiation asset on the page. It justifies the premium price point and creates a clear "reason to upgrade" that the competition can't match.
→ Current IM8 strength: keep and improve, don't remove
18
Accordions — Compressed, Alphabetically Logical
MEDIUM
Keep accordions for FAQ, Benefits, Ingredients, Science, How to Use, Shipping. Reduce the labels to short, scannable titles. Remove "Tasting Notes" as a standalone section — fold it into Product Description. Remove anything that's not genuinely needed to close a sale or reduce a return.
All 6 competitors use accordions for detailed product information below the CTA. This is the universal pattern — buy first, read the details second. The important thing is what appears before the accordions. AG1 Grüns Everyday Dose DIRTEA
19
Stack Upsell — Post-Information, Framed as Upgrade
HIGH
Add a "Upgrade Your Stack" upsell section after the accordions. Show one product card (AM + PM Stack) with a strong savings badge. Use purple/different accent colour to visually differentiate from the main purchase flow. Include a CTA: "UPGRADE & SAVE 30%."
DIRTEA has an "Upgrade To A Stack" section with a compelling £81 → £57 offer. AG1 has "Complete Your Wellness Stack" with companion product add-ons. Upsells placed after the main information block have higher conversion than those placed before the primary CTA. DIRTEA AG1
20
Review Cards + Bottom Repeat CTA
MEDIUM
Show 2 representative review cards (5-star, specific outcome, verified buyer + location). Then repeat the primary CTA in full at the very bottom of the page with the money-back guarantee line and payment method icons. Any user who reads to the bottom is highly interested — make sure there's a clear call to action waiting for them.
AG1 repeats its full checkout CTA ("Checkout · Save 10%") at the bottom with payment method icons. The bottom CTA serves users who scroll all the way through and need one more nudge. Without it, a motivated buyer has to scroll back to the top. AG1
★
Critical Fix: Currency Consistency
CRITICAL
The current IM8 hero image references "$72 in Welcome Gifts" while all pricing is in GBP. This inconsistency creates subconscious friction and undermines trust. Either update the hero image asset to reference "£72" for UK visitors, or use dynamic currency rendering based on geolocation. Never mix $ and £ on the same page for the same market.
Currency mismatch is a classic conversion killer in international DTC. UK visitors see "$72" and instinctively question whether they're on the right version of the site, or whether the pricing applies to them.
→ Current IM8 bug: hero image says "$72" but pricing shows GBP
